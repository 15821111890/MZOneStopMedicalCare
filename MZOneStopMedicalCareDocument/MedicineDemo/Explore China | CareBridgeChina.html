Shanghai Highlights

China's most cosmopolitan city—where ancient tradition meets futuristic innovation

The Bund

Historic waterfront promenade lined with colonial-era architecture. The best place to witness Shanghai's dramatic skyline contrast—classic European buildings on one side, futuristic skyscrapers on the other.

Shanghai Tower

At 632 meters, the world's second-tallest building. Visit the observation deck on the 118th floor for breathtaking 360° views of the city and the winding Huangpu River below.

Yu Garden

A 400-year-old classical Chinese garden featuring traditional pavilions, rockeries, ponds, and ancient trees. Adjacent to the bustling Yuyuan Bazaar for traditional crafts and local snacks.

French Concession

Tree-lined avenues, charming villas, and boutique cafes. This former French settlement offers a peaceful retreat with its European architecture, art galleries, and trendy restaurants.

Huangpu River Cruise

See Shanghai's iconic skyline illuminated at night. The cruise offers the most spectacular views of the Bund and Pudong's glittering skyscrapers reflecting on the water.

Culinary Delights

From soup dumplings (xiaolongbao) at Michelin-starred restaurants to street food at night markets. Shanghai's diverse food scene reflects its history as a global trading port.
